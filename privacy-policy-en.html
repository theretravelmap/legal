Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 12, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how There: Your travel map (the "App") handles information when you use it.

1. Personal Data Collected

The App does not require you to create an account and does not include its own developer-operated backend for storing your travel data.

Based on the current implementation, the developer does not collect analytics data, advertising identifiers, or tracking data through the App. If you contact the developer by email, the developer will receive your email address and the contents of your message.

2. Data You May Store in the App

The App allows you to store certain information on your device, including:

  • Countries you mark as visited.
  • The date each country was marked as visited.
  • Your chosen display name.
  • An optional profile photo that you choose through the system photo picker.

This information is used only to provide the App's features, such as your travel map, profile display, and statistics.

3. Local Storage

The App uses SwiftData to store visited countries, your display name, and your optional profile photo locally on your device.

The code currently reviewed does not show CloudKit or iCloud sync being enabled for this data. Your information may still be included in Apple device backups if backup features are enabled on your device, under Apple's own terms and privacy practices.

4. Photos

Adding a profile photo is optional. If you choose a photo through the system photo picker, the selected image data may be stored in the App's local data so it can be shown in your profile.

The developer does not receive your photo through the App.

5. Network Requests and Third-Party Services

Most core travel-map data shown in the App is bundled with the App itself. When you open a country details screen, the App may request country statistics from the World Bank public API.

Those requests are sent directly from your device to that third-party service. They may include technical information normally sent in web requests, such as your IP address and device networking metadata, under that service's own policies.

The App can also open external links such as Apple Maps, Google Maps, Waze, Instagram, TikTok, email links, and web pages for legal documents. If you open those links, the privacy practices of those services apply.

6. No Analytics, Ads, or Tracking

Based on the current codebase, the App does not include third-party analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, or app-level tracking technologies.

The developer does not receive usage analytics about how you mark countries, browse the map, or use the App's screens.

7. Accounts, Notifications, and Purchases

The current implementation does not provide user accounts or sign-in.

The current implementation also does not show local or push notifications, and it does not show implemented in-app purchases or subscriptions.

Apple may still process information related to App Store distribution, downloads, reviews, or payments under Apple's own policies where applicable.

8. Data Retention

Data stored by the App remains on your device until you change it, delete it, or remove the App.

If you email the developer, the developer may keep that correspondence for as long as reasonably necessary to respond and maintain basic records.

9. Your Privacy Rights

Because the App mainly stores data locally on your device, you can usually access, update, or delete that data directly in the App or by deleting the App from your device.

If applicable law gives you additional privacy rights, you may also contact the developer at beucaalexandru@icloud.com.

10. Children's Privacy

The App is not designed to intentionally collect personal data from children. The developer does not knowingly collect children's personal data through the App.

If you believe a child has sent personal information to the developer by email, contact the developer so appropriate steps can be taken.

11. Security

Data stored by the App relies on the security protections provided by iOS and your device, including device-level access controls and Apple's system security measures.

No method of storage or transmission can be guaranteed to be completely secure, but the App is designed to minimize unnecessary data sharing.

12. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect changes to the App or legal requirements.

When this page is updated, the "Last updated" date at the top will also be updated.
